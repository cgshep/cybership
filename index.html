Workshop itinerary

2-hour CyberShip session plan

Built for charities, those interested in start-ups, and people running a startup, SME, or microbusiness. The format blends threat awareness, hands-on demonstrations, and an action planning exercise.

Upcoming locations

  • 13 Feb 2026, 2–4pm – Frederick Douglas Centre, Helix Campus, Westgate Road, Newcastle-upon-Tyne
  • 20 Feb 2026, 2–4pm – Henry Daysh Building nr. Great North Museum: Hancock, Newcastle-Upon-Tyne

Sign up form
12:00 – 12:10

Arrival, registration, baseline

Welcome, refreshments, and a quick baseline questionnaire to capture confidence and status.

12:10 – 12:20

SME threat landscape & emerging threats

Scene-setting on why SMEs are targeted, common attack types, and how generative AI amplifies phishing and fraud.

12:20 – 1:15

Live attack demonstrations

  • AI-assisted phishing and account compromise
  • Password reuse and credential attacks
  • Public Wi-Fi (evil twin) interception
  • Ransomware impact simulation
1:15 – 1:30

From attacks to controls

Map each demo to Cyber Essentials controls and share safe AI usage guidance.

1:30 – 1:50

SME action planning exercise

Participants tick current controls, circle three priority actions, and share one planned change.

1:50 – 2:00

Q&A and post-session survey

Capture intended actions, AI usage, and feedback for future support.
